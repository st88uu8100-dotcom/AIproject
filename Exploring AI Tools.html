Exploring AI Tools

This phase was not about mastery. It was about curiosity, experimentation, and learning what AI could actually do.

Starting With Confusion

At first, AI felt overwhelming. There were too many tools, too many promises, and no clear direction. I didn’t know which ones mattered — or how they could realistically help me.

Testing Without Expectations

I stopped trying to “make money” immediately and focused on testing instead. I explored text generation, image creation, and automation tools just to see what was possible.

Understanding Strengths and Limits

Not every tool was useful. Some saved time, others created noise. This stage taught me that AI is powerful — but only when paired with clear intent.

Connecting Tools to Real Problems

The breakthrough came when I stopped asking “What can AI do?” and started asking “What problems already exist that AI can solve faster or cheaper?”

From Tools to Systems

AI stopped being a collection of apps. It became a workflow — a system that supported ideas, execution, and iteration.
