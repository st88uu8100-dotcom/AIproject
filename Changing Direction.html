Changing Direction

When I realized my first approach wasn’t getting traction, I had to rethink everything — and it started with looking beyond my own country.

Being an Ordinary Student in Korea

I’m just a regular high school student living in Korea. At first, I thought, “Why not just focus on Korea? It’s my home, I know the culture, and I can reach local users easily.”

Facing the Limits of the Korean Market

But soon I realized the challenges. Korea is a small country, and people there only pay attention to what’s fun, trendy, or instantly entertaining. No matter how much I promoted my site locally, it didn’t attract interest because it wasn’t flashy or immediately engaging.

Rethinking the Approach

I started thinking: maybe I need a bigger audience. Maybe I need to show my work to people outside Korea. This shift in perspective opened the door to new possibilities — reaching communities abroad where my ideas could actually gain traction.
