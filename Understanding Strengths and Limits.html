Understanding Strengths and Limits

The first step wasn’t about mastering AI — it was about knowing what I didn’t know.

Learning by Asking

When I started my site, I didn’t have all the answers. I realized quickly that I couldn’t do everything alone. My coding skills were basic, design sense limited, and I had zero marketing experience.

So I asked AI. Not for shortcuts, but for guidance on things I didn’t understand. “How do I set up hosting?” “What’s the best way to structure pages for clarity?” “How can I make this content readable and engaging?”

AI became a mirror — showing me where my knowledge ended and where I needed to focus. Every time I hit a wall, I asked, learned, implemented, and repeated.

I discovered that my strengths weren’t technical perfection or knowing all the answers. They were curiosity, persistence, and the ability to learn quickly from tools around me.

At the same time, I accepted my limits: some tasks were tedious, some decisions required expertise I didn’t yet have, and some errors were inevitable.

Recognizing both strengths and limits helped me structure my workflow, focus my energy, and trust that the AI-assisted approach could carry me farther than brute force alone ever could.
